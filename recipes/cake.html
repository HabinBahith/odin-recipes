Cake

Cake is a baker's confectionery[1] made by combining flour with a liquid, a fat or both, and usually with a leavening agent. Cakes are usually baked, but can be griddled. In their oldest forms, cakes were modifications of bread, but cakes now cover a wide range of preparations that can be simple or elaborate and which share features with other desserts such as pastries, meringues, custards, and pies.

ingridients

  • flour
  • Sugar
  • Eggs
  • Fat: Full-fat milk
  • Leavening: Baking powder

steps

  1. Preheat and Prep: Preheat the oven to 350°F and prepare cake pans by spraying with cooking spray and lining bottoms with parchment paper
  2. Cream Butter and Sugar: Beat unsalted butter and granulated sugar together until light and fluffy, approximately 3 minutes.
  3. Add Wet Ingredients: Mix in eggs one at a time, followed by vanilla extract and sour cream (or milk), beating until combined
  4. Combine Dry and Wet: Alternately add dry ingredients (flour, baking powder, salt) and milk to the butter mixture, mixing on low speed until just combined
  5. Bake: Pour batter into pans and bake for 18-22 minutes for 4-inch pans or 24-28 minutes for 6-inch pans, until a toothpick inserted comes out clean
  6. Cool: Let the cake cool in pans for 10 minutes, then remove to wire racks to cool completely before frosting.
