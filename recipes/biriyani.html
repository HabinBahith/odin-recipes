Biriyani


Biryani is a popular layered mixed rice dish originating from South Asia, traditionally made with basmati rice, meat (such as chicken, goat, or beef), seafood, or vegetables, and a complex blend of aromatic spices


Ingridients

  • Base: Aged basmati rice
  • Protein: Traditionally chicken, mutton, lamb, or fish
  • Spices: A mix of whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves, cumin) and powdered spices (turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder)
  • Aromatics & Marinade: Yogurt (curd) to tenderize the meat, along with ginger-garlic paste, onions, and fresh herbs like mint and coriander
  • Fats & Garnishes: Ghee (clarified butter) is used for richness, while saffron (often soaked in milk) adds color and fragrance. Fried onions (birista), raisins, and nuts (cashews, almonds) are common garnishes

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken: Combine bone-in chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala or biryani masala, lemon juice, salt, and fresh herbs (mint and cilantro). Marinate for at least 2 hours, or overnight in the refrigerator for tender, flavorful meat.
  2. Prepare the Rice: Wash aged basmati rice until the water runs clear and soak it for 30–40 minutes. In a large pot of boiling water, add whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaf), salt, and oil. Add the drained rice and cook until 75% done (grainy with a slight bite), then drain immediately
  3. 3. Cook the Masala and Onions: Fry sliced onions in oil or ghee until golden brown and crisp to create birista. In the same heavy-bottomed pot, sauté the marinated chicken (and any additional onion paste or tomatoes if using a simpler method) until the oil separates and the masala is well-cooked.
  4. Layer the Biryani: Spread the cooked chicken masala at the bottom of the pot. Layer half of the parboiled rice on top, followed by fried onions, mint, cilantro, saffron milk, and ghee. Add the remaining rice and repeat the garnishes
  5. Seal and Steam (Dum): Seal the pot tightly with a damp cloth or dough under the lid to trap steam. Cook on high heat for 2–3 minutes, then lower to the lowest flame and cook for 15–20 minutes. Let it rest off the heat for 20 minutes before gently fluffing and serving with raita
